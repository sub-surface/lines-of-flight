Lines of Flight

A dot that stays. A line that leaves.

“A line of flight is not made of two points. It passes between points, it comes up through the middle.”

— Gilles Deleuze & Félix Guattari, A Thousand Plateaus

A black dot sits in the middle of a white screen. That is the whole world, at first.

You move, and a trail follows — not a cursor, but a living line you have drawn into being. The line is not kept. It is ink-matter, carried by an invisible field, that diffuses into weather and fades into white. The field reveals itself only in how the ink bends.

Two beings

The Dot Stillpoint. Rooted, sedentary — the one who stays so that there is a here at all. It cannot follow you, and knows it. You cannot flee from nowhere.
The Line You, becoming. Where the Dot is a point, the Line is a passage — never finished, always in the middle of something. The long conversation is between staying and leaving.

How to be here

There is nothing to win and nothing to lose. Stillness is not punished; it is the pause. Move slowly and watch the world breathe.

A voice in the white

The world carries a voice — a shuffled deck of aphorisms drawn from Deleuze: A Thousand Plateaus, Difference and Repetition, The Logic of Sense, What is Philosophy?, and his readings of Spinoza, Bergson, and Blanchot. The words do not print — they precipitate, settling into the empty quarters of the screen as ink held briefly to its own shape, then released to the field as weather. The deck reshuffles and loops, so the world never goes silent.

Worn lightly

The philosophy is the grain of the wood, not the carving. The words rhizome, deterritorialization, assemblage never appear on screen. The ideas arrive as physics and feeling: a line of flight is the ink that escapes the point it started from; smooth and striated space are the open field and the ruled well; an assemblage is the moment a connection makes a third thing that neither was alone.

Made of

One velocity field, evaluated once per frame onto a coarse grid and read by thousands of ink-particles with mass, momentum, and age. No build step, no dependencies, no backend — a single HTML file and an HTML5 canvas, served from the edge. The full design and engineering notes live in the spec.
